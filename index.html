Vajont Dam Disaster (1963)

This simulation recreates the geological conditions that led to one of history's worst dam disasters. On October 9, 1963, a massive landslide from Mount Toc entered the Vajont reservoir in northern Italy, creating a tsunami that killed nearly 2,000 people.

Key Elements

  • Mount Toc: The unstable mountain with layers of limestone and clay
  • Clay Layer: The weak geological layer along which failure occurred
  • Reservoir: The Vajont reservoir that saturated the mountain
  • Arch Dam: The 262m tall concrete dam that survived the disaster

The Disaster Progression

  1. Rising reservoir levels saturated the mountain's porous limestone
  2. Water pressure built up along a prehistoric clay layer
  3. After heavy rainfall, the entire mountainside (270 million m³) slid into the reservoir
  4. The displaced water created a 250m wave that overtopped the dam

Controlling the Simulation

• Click and drag to orbit the scene

• Scroll to zoom in/out

• Adjust water level with the slider

• Use view buttons for different perspectives

• Play the complete disaster sequence to see the events unfold
